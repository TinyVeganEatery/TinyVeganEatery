Zesty Tofu Balls in Sweet & Sour Sauce: A Flavor-Packed Vegan Delight

By Yasmin
September 18, 2024

Looking for a delicious vegan recipe that's bursting with flavor? These crispy tofu balls in sweet and sour sauce are the perfect combination of tangy, sweet, and savory, making them a go-to dish for any occasion. Whether you're a long-time vegan or just looking to add more plant-based meals to your diet, this easy recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings.

Made from protein-rich tofu, these tofu balls are baked or air-fried to golden perfection, then tossed in a homemade sweet and sour sauce that strikes the perfect balance between sweet, tangy, and salty. This dish pairs beautifully with rice, making it a wholesome and filling meal. Plus, it’s customizable, so you can tweak the sweetness, sourness, or spice level to suit your taste!

Not only is this recipe simple to make, but it’s also a great option for meal prep, and it’s gluten-free if you use tamari in place of soy sauce. Let’s dive into how you can make this flavorful vegan dish!

Ingredients for Tofu Balls:
600 grams of hard tofu
4 crushed garlic cloves
1 tablespoon of salt
1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
1/2 teaspoon of sweet paprika
2 tablespoons of olive oil

How to Make the Tofu Balls:
Prepare the Tofu Mixture:
Start by breaking up the tofu into chunks and adding it to a food processor. Toss in your garlic, salt, black pepper, sweet paprika, and olive oil. Pulse everything together until the mixture reaches a smooth yet crumbly texture. You want it to hold together when forming into balls but not be overly processed.

Form the Balls:
Roll the tofu mixture into small, bite-sized balls. For an extra crispy exterior, brush some olive oil over the balls.

Bake or Air-Fry:
Arrange the tofu balls in a single layer on a tray or air fryer basket. Spray the top lightly with olive oil. Bake at 180°C (turbo mode if available) for about 30 minutes, or until the tofu balls are golden brown and firm on the outside.

Sweet & Sour Sauce Ingredients:
5 tablespoons soy sauce or Tamari 
1/4 cup of your choice of sweetener (maple syrup, silan, or agave)
1/8 to 1/4 cup of lemon juice (adjust to your preferred sourness level)
1 tablespoon of sesame oil
2 tablespoons tomato paste
1/4 cup water (to thin the sauce slightly)
Optional: 3 crushed garlic cloves and 1/2 teaspoon of grated ginger
(Tip: If you’re using garlic and ginger, lightly sauté them in oil before adding to the sauce for extra depth of flavor.)

How to Make the Sauce:
Mix the Ingredients:
In a jar or small bowl, combine the soy sauce, sweetener, lemon juice, sesame oil, and tomato paste. Add a little water to thin the mixture to your liking. If you're adding garlic and ginger, make sure to sauté them first, then add them to the sauce mix.

Simmer and Taste:
Pour the sauce mixture into a sauté pan and let it simmer for about 5 minutes to thicken and intensify the flavors. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. You can balance the sauce with more sweetness, saltiness, or sourness based on your preference.

Putting It All Together:
Coat the Tofu Balls in Sauce:
Once the tofu balls are baked or air-fried to perfection, add them directly to the sauce pan. Gently toss them in the sauce until they are fully coated and have absorbed all that sweet and sour goodness.


Serve and Garnish:
Serve these saucy tofu balls over a bed of steamed rice for a comforting meal. For an extra pop of flavor and texture, top them with roasted sesame seeds, freshly chopped coriander, or green onions.

Tips for Success:
Air Fryer Option: If you have an air fryer, it’s a great alternative to baking. It gives the tofu balls a crispier exterior in less time.
Customizable Sweetness: Depending on your preferred level of sweetness, feel free to experiment with different types of sweeteners like maple syrup, silan, or agave.
Meal Prep Friendly: These tofu balls keep well in the fridge, making them a perfect meal-prep option. Just store the balls and sauce separately and combine them when ready to serve.

Enjoy your delicious homemade tofu balls with sweet and sour sauce!
